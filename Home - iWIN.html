What is iWIN

Integrated Women in Health Network (iWIN) is Jhpiego’s flagship Maternal, Newborn and Child Health (MNCH) initiative supported by Takeda Global CSR. Through this intervention, Jhpiego is providing strategic and catalytic support to the Government of Madhya Pradesh in strengthening the existing capacities of the healthcare systems for comprehensive antenatal, intrapartum and postpartum care. iWIN would establish quality management systems in both programs and technology spheres to ensure Client-Centered, Continuous and Coordinated Care (5C) for every mother and newborn in the intervention geography.

Revival of Data tracking mechanism in Labour room

I-WIN team took up the challenge of rejuvenating inactive technology systems in MP and ensured strong advocacy and technical support for the re-activation and usage of labour room technology platforms called ASMAN that were piloted by Jhpiego back in 2020.

Putting back ASMAN system running was key to I-WIN as it helped us by learning from our earlier experiences/mistakes, leveraging earlier investments, has also showed us nuances of technology operations and gave confidence to the team and to the government that digital solutions in complex public health world are possible.

In last six months, in a systematic manner, team addressed hardware fixation, software updates and staff capacity building and motivation as well. Dedicated cadre of Tech Consultants was deployed for immediate troubleshooting of issues reported in the system and that really helped in maintaining motivation of users of the system. Program Officers and Coordinators posted under I-WIN project from 4 districts are providing supportive supervision to all facilities and ensuring optimum utilization of this application system.

ASMAN is playing key role inmoving towards I-WIN objective of creating end to tracking of maternity event. The Clinical Decision Support system in ASMAN is also helping in ensuring improved adherence to evidenced based practices during intrapartum and immediate postpartum care. ASMAN is not enough but is definitely a crucial piece in the puzzle that I-WIN is trying to solve.

Innovative approach to build capacity of ANM through capsules (interactive learning modules)

Auxillary Nurse Midwives (ANM) play the central role in delivery of MNCH services in India. They are responsible for comprehensive Antenatal Care (ANC) and Postnatal Care (PNC); early detection of high risks and timely referral of women with complications for further management.

During landscaping and baseline assessment under I-WIN, we observed major gaps in basic knowledge and competency elements around key practices. ANMs get capacity building opportunity only during their induction course but have little ongoing refreshers or mentorship within the system.

Based on the Low Dose High Frequency approach, an interactive short (60-90 minutes) capsular learning packages were created and delivered through participatory learning methodologies. Team has successfully rolled out three capsules on three different topics for 200 ANMs in six intervention districts within the reporting period. The effect of capsular content delivery was reinforced with useful Job Aids developed and distributed by the team. Overall response from the ANMs is encouraging and can be summarized with a quote from an ANM from Ujjain district,“I am enjoying the capsule training a lot because the method of training is really entertaining and interactive, just in 1-2 hours I learnt so much. The job aids given are also very helpful. Thanks.”

Care Navigators: Youth on bike and with mobile saving lives!

In initial months of I-WIN implementation, during series of field visits and interactions with HCPs, we used to observe youth from the same village in and around ANC service delivery points, immunization points, offering their help and assistance to needy clients as and when required on a voluntary basis.

Most of them qualified, smartphone enabled, bike riders but unfortunately unemployed. In order to utilize these locally available village experts, I-WIN team decided to onboard and formalize their support in a more structured manner. We onboarded them as Care Navigators in our initial focused implementation geography and utilized their presence for field-level support to ANMs.

Support was mainly towards improvement of ANC session organization, resources availability, use of digital applications for recording and effective referrals in case required. Simple, feasible checklist and tools were developed and care navigators are provided with training and support to ensure standardized nature of non-technical assistance that they provide to ANMs.

In identified 6 focused intervention blocks, we have 32 Care Navigators as on date such that each one is supporting 25-30 ANMs in a structured manner. They have already supported navigation of more than 1000 pregnant women across care pathways. In the words of Block program manager of Segaon, Khargoan ,“With help of care navigators we are able to identify the issues with ANMOL and referral, now we are able to follow up these issues on time!”
